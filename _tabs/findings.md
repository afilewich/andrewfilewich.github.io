# Findings

## Overview

The project found that students who responded to the survey generally viewed the pre-laboratory videos positively, especially in relation to preparation, procedural clarity, confidence, and skill performance.

## Video Viewership

Student uptake of the videos was limited across the semester. Verified unique views declined from the first video module to the fourth, suggesting that overall engagement with the resource decreased over time.

## Survey Responses

Among survey respondents, the strongest patterns were seen in:

- preparation
- confidence
- clarity
- skill support

These findings suggest that the videos were most useful as practical preparation tools before entering the laboratory environment.

## Open-Ended Feedback

Students described several benefits of the videos, including:

- better procedural clarity
- helpful visual demonstrations
- improved role preparation
- the ability to replay the videos when needed

Reported challenges were limited, but some students noted that:

- they were not always aware that the videos were available
- some video content did not fully align with what happened in the laboratory

## Main Interpretation

A key finding of the project was the gap between **positive perceptions** and **low uptake**.

In other words, students who used the videos often found them helpful, but the videos were not used consistently across the class as a whole.

## Why This Matters

This suggests that educational resources should not be judged only by whether they are useful in principle, but also by whether students are aware of them, can access them easily, and are encouraged to use them as part of normal course workflow.
