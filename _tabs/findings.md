# Findings

## Overview

This project found that students who responded to the survey generally viewed the pre-laboratory videos positively, especially in relation to preparation, procedural clarity, confidence, and skill performance.

## Video Viewership

Student uptake of the videos was limited across the semester. Verified unique views declined across the four modules:

- Laboratory 1: 31 views (37.3%)
- Laboratory 2: 29 views (34.9%)
- Laboratory 3: 21 views (25.3%)
- Laboratory 4: 14 views (16.9%)

This showed that no video reached even 40% of the enrolled class.

## Survey Responses

Five students completed the end-of-term survey. Responses were generally neutral to positive, with the strongest patterns appearing in:

- preparation
- confidence
- clarity
- skill support

At the regrouped category level, the descriptive mean scores were:

- Preparation: 3.90
- Confidence: 4.00
- Clarity: 4.00
- Skill: 4.00
- Learning: 3.60
- Engagement: 3.87
- Comparison: 3.80

## Open-Ended Feedback

Students described several benefits of the videos, including:

- better procedural clarity
- stronger role preparation
- helpful visual demonstrations
- the ability to replay the videos when needed

Reported challenges were limited, but students noted that:

- some videos did not always align perfectly with laboratory activities
- some students were not aware the videos were available

## Main Interpretation

A key finding of the project was the gap between **positive perceptions** and **low uptake**.

In other words, students who used the videos often found them helpful, but the videos were not used consistently across the class as a whole.

## Why This Matters

This suggests that educational resources should not be judged only by whether they are useful in principle, but also by whether students are aware of them, can access them easily, and are encouraged to use them as part of normal course workflow.
