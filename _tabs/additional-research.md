# Additional Research

## Co-Authored Publication

**“You Admit a Resident, You Admit a Family”: The Impact of COVID-19 Restrictions on Family Time in Long-Term Care**

Christianson, T., Appiah-Kusi, E., Bremner, J., Filewich, A., Qazi, A., & Reid, C. (2023). *Journal of Aging and Long-Term Care, 6*(3), 139–157.  
[https://doi.org/10.51819/jaltc.2023.1353633](https://doi.org/10.51819/jaltc.2023.1353633)

## Overview

In addition to my honours thesis, I contributed to this co-authored publication examining how COVID-19 visitation restrictions affected families with loved ones living in long-term care in British Columbia.

## Study Focus

The paper explored how families perceived the impact of pandemic-related restrictions on family time, quality of care, communication, and overall well-being.

## Study Design

This publication reports qualitative findings from semi-structured interviews with family members as part of a larger mixed-methods study.

## Main Themes

The paper identified five interconnected themes:

- quality of life
- quality of care
- mental health concerns
- communication
- the rules

The rules functioned as the overarching theme connecting the others.

## Key Takeaway

The paper argues that family caregivers are not simply visitors. They are important members of the care team, and long-term care policies should be more family-centered and should include families in decision-making.

## Why I Included It Here

Although this portfolio is centered on my honours thesis in kinesiology education, this publication reflects another important part of my academic work: applied health research, family-centered care, and the human impact of health policy.
