## Title

**Pre-Laboratory Instructional Videos in Experiential Learning: A Kinesiology Perspective**

## Overview

This project examined whether short pre-laboratory instructional videos could support student preparation, procedural clarity, confidence, and skill development in undergraduate kinesiology laboratory courses.

## Why This Matters

Laboratory-based learning is central to kinesiology education, but students often need to learn unfamiliar procedures, anatomical landmarks, equipment use, and testing protocols under time pressure. My thesis explored whether short multimedia videos could help students prepare more effectively before entering the lab.

## Research Questions

1. How did students perceive the influence of pre-laboratory video viewing on preparedness, confidence, procedural understanding, learning efficiency, and skill self-efficacy?

2. What patterns of pre-laboratory video use, reported benefits, challenges, and suggestions emerged across the semester?

## Study Context

This project was conducted in Kinesiology 375 — Tests and Measurements at the University of Calgary. Four short pre-laboratory videos were created for the first four lab sessions and distributed through the course shell.

## Key Finding

Among survey respondents, the videos were perceived most positively for preparation, procedural clarity, confidence, and skill performance, although overall student uptake of the videos across the class remained low.
