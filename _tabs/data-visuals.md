## Overview

This page presents selected quantitative results from my honours thesis in a more compact and visual format.

The goal of this section is to make the findings easier to interpret while also serving as the foundation for a more technical and programmatic portfolio component.

## Core Dataset Used on This Page

The visual summaries on this page are based on two main result sources from the project:

- verified unique views across the four pre-laboratory video modules
- regrouped descriptive mean scores from the end-of-term survey

## Video Viewership

The pre-laboratory videos were viewed by a limited proportion of the class, and viewership declined across the semester.

Raw verified unique views:

- Lab 1: 31
- Lab 2: 29
- Lab 3: 21
- Lab 4: 14

These values represent the number of students who accessed each video module.

## Descriptive Category Means

Survey responses were also summarized into broader descriptive categories.

Category means:

- Preparation: 3.90
- Confidence: 4.00
- Clarity: 4.00
- Skill: 4.00
- Learning: 3.60
- Engagement: 3.87
- Comparison: 3.80

![Descriptive Mean Scores by Category](/assets/img/thesis/category_means_chart.png)

_Programmatically generated chart showing descriptive mean scores across regrouped survey categories._

## Interpretation

The viewership data suggest that optional pre-laboratory video use declined over time, even though the survey results among respondents were generally positive.

This supports one of the key interpretations of the project: the videos appeared useful to students who used them, but uptake across the class remained limited.
