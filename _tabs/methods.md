# Methods

## Study Design

This project used an exploratory mixed-methods design. Quantitative data came from descriptive survey items and video viewership analytics, while qualitative data came from open-ended survey responses.

## Participants

The project took place in Kinesiology 375 — Tests and Measurements at the University of Calgary. A total of 83 students were enrolled in the course. Students in the course were in the second, third, or fourth year of study.

## Course Context

The course included lectures and five laboratory sessions spaced throughout the semester. Laboratory sessions gave students opportunities to practice strength, health, and physiological testing procedures on classmates.

## Multimedia Intervention

Four short pre-laboratory instructional videos were created for the first four laboratory sessions. Each video was designed to align with course pre-reading and to provide concise demonstrations of key procedures.

The video topics were:

- Jackson-Pollock skinfold testing
- Heart rate, blood pressure, and mCAFT
- CSEP PATH test battery
- Musculoskeletal health testing and appraisal

## Data Collection

At the end of the term, students were invited to complete a voluntary mixed-methods survey about their experience with the videos. Video platform analytics were also used to track verified unique views for each module.

## Data Analysis

Because the survey response rate was low, the data were analyzed descriptively. Likert-style responses were summarized using counts, means, and observed ranges. Open-ended responses were grouped into recurring themes using inductive content analysis.

## Why This Method Was Used

This design allowed the project to examine not only whether students viewed the videos positively, but also how often the videos were actually used and what implementation issues emerged across the semester.
